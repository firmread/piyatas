]]> http://piyatas.tothong.com/glai-behind-the-scene/feed/ 0 TWO behind the Scene http://piyatas.tothong.com/two-behind-the-scene/ http://piyatas.tothong.com/two-behind-the-scene/#respond Tue, 01 Jan 2013 14:29:15 +0000 http://piyatas.tothong.com/?p=754 TWO is a midterm project for Advance Interactive class. The concept is inspired from the views I have and had seen from the windows in my rooms in Thailand and USA. My “20% of work” is to illustrate the differences between two cities, Bangkok and New York. The “80%” is to let the spectators notice and think of the changes which will be occurred while they walk pass the two big scenes on the wall.

This is the first draft of TWO.

At first, I intended to display the illustrations on the window, that is going to hang in the middle of an empty room. I would take the pane of glass out and replace it with the white board. The illustration of Bangkok would be projected on one side of the white board and New York would be on another side.

I was thinking more thoroughly about input and output. Whenever spectators come by to see my illustrations, some parts of it will be added or subtracted or changed. Besides the visual part, sound effects would also be part of the output. In this process, I made up my mind that my illustration style would be like a child drawing on the wall.

The rough sketch of my illustrations. I drew bridges, small houses, Wat Phra Kaew (famous Thai temples), Tuk-Tuk (Thai motor-cyle), the sun, a flock of birds, Manhattan bridge, taxi and hotdog man. I tried to sketch the architectural places, transportations, animals and many other things I found interesting to me and representative these two cities.

This is the timeline of the output. I paired up the different things, for instance, Phra Ram 8 bridge + Manhattan bridge, Tuk-Tuk + NYC cab, the sun + the moon, a kite + a balloon.

I also combined these opposites by making up the stories as if they can happen at the same time, for example;

The sun is rising in Bangkok, while the moon is shining in New York.
Tuk-Tuk is running from Bangkok and moving onto Manhattan bridge.
New York cab is running from New York and moving onto Phra Ram 8 bridge.
A flock of birds are flying away from electricity posts in Bangkok to skyscrapers in New York.

I animated these stories in Flash Action Script 3.0 and export them as vdo clips.

I use MaxMSP as my source of code to accomplish this interactive project.

My input is the movement of the spectators. Based on the fact that they can walk pass the illustrations from left to right or right to left. I divided motion detection into 6 parts vertically. Each part controls different vdo clips of the illustrations.

Two framed pictures hanging on the wall

A flock of birds flying from Bangkok into New York picture, if a spectator stands in front of electric posts in Bangkok picture.
On the other hand, they will fly from New York into Bangkok picture, if a spectator stands in front of skyscrapers in New York picture.

If spectators move to the middle of Bangkok picture, Tuk-Tuk will run out of frame and go to Manhattan bridge.
Whereas, a cab will run out of frame and go to Phra Ram 8 bridge, if spectators move to the middle of New York picture.

The sun rises and the moon shines, whenever a spectator stands in front of them.

Nevertheless, everything can happen at the same time, if there are many spectators viewing the pictures.

I choose a hallway as a location of TWO because people walk pass this wall naturally. This behavior will trigger the animated vdo clips automatically.

For the technical parts, I used 1 projector, 1 uni-brain camera and 1 computer. I installed the projector on the high position. As a result, it will prevent shadows which can be casted on the wall when people were walking by or getting close to the pictures. I set the camera in the same level of human body so it can easily detect people movements.
